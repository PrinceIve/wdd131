Naija Street Bites - Site Plan

Site Name

Naija Street Bites - A celebration of Nigeria’s most loved street foods.

Reason for Name: "Naija Street Bites" was chosen to reflect the cultural richness and everyday nature of street food in Nigeria. The name is catchy, easy to remember, and connects immediately with the target audience who are familiar with "Naija" as a popular local name for Nigeria.

Optional Domain: naijastreetbites.com

Site Purpose

The purpose of Naija Street Bites is to spotlight the diverse world of Nigerian street foods through visually engaging content, dish ratings, interactive maps, and user-submitted suggestions. It aims to:

  • Educate local and global audiences on Nigerian street food culture.
  • Serve as a guide for locating popular street foods.
  • Allow users to interact by rating dishes and contributing ideas.

Scenarios

  • “Where can I find the best suya near me in Lagos?”
  • “What's the difference between puff-puff and akara, and how are they made?”
  • “Are there street foods that are vegan-friendly or gluten-free?”
  • “Can I submit a street food vendor I discovered in Port Harcourt?”

Color Schema

  • Primary Color – Tomato Red (#E63946): Used for headers, buttons, and call-to-action sections to represent vibrance and appetite.
  • Secondary Color – Warm Beige (#FAF3E0): Used for backgrounds and content areas to create contrast and maintain readability.

Typography

  • Heading Font: "Poppins", sans-serif – Used for all headers and navigation text. Clean and bold.
  • Body Font: "Open Sans", sans-serif – Used for body text, paragraphs, and forms. Simple and readable.
